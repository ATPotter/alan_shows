SABOS was doing Oliver, and I really couldn't face the prospect of another child-centred musical. So instead I went along to my local society, Falkirk Operatic Society.

I had hoped that I would be able to get the part of Francis Fryer - my get-up for the auditions was something to behold - but in the end I was delighted to be given the part of Danny Gilmartin.

The show was great fun; I thoroughly doing a local show.

Photographic Notes

This was the first time that my show pictures were taken with a digital camera. Most of them were shot with a Fujifilm S7000 with a hotshoe mounted flashgun, although some were taken without flash using a Canon Ixus camera. The flashgun offered no manual control, so it was either too bright or it was switched off!

The Ixus, on the other hand, was fired with no flash, and it did produce a few very good pictures.

My friend who shot with the S7000 remarked that the zoom sometimes "stuck". A few weeks later, I was shooting a show and found exactly the same. I returned it to the manufacturer and was given a refund.

That left the question of how to replace it. I didn't have the money for an SLR for shows and a regular camera for holidays, so I bought a Canon Powershot Pro1. It was a gorgeous camera, which took first-class low-ISO pictures until it died in a Taiwanese tropical storm. But it didn't have the high-ISO performance of an SLR, and so it would still be a good while before I was able to ditch the flashgun altogether...
