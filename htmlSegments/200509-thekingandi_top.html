In June 2005, I was a regular participant on the Amatuer Musicals forums at Amdram.co.uk. I still visit quite frequently - it's a great place to hang out.

Anyway, one of the other participants was Pamela Jarvis, who was organising a production of The King And I. And her King had been forced to drop out, for personal reasons. Would I be able to learn the part of "The King" in six weeks (including one week being away on holiday?

I have to say, it wasn't terribly easy. There is a lot of dialogue. But somehow I managed to get the words pretty close to correct!

The production was in the "Performance Space" of the Tolbooth in Stirling. This is somewhere around a 150-seater, with the audience's seats coming right to the floor area that is used as a stage. Indeed, some nights I was sitting right beside audience members during the Act Two ballet! It was also small enough that we didn't need microphones, and the two-piano accompaniment sounded wonderful in that intimate environment.

I really did enjoy this show, hard work though it was.

My wife brought the children to see the Saturday matinée show. At the interval, I am told, she took my daughter to the bathroom. Jenni (the daughter) started crying that she wasn't wanting to leave the ladies' loos. "I don't want daddy to die," she wailed (she had been primed that that was going to happen at the end of the show). My wife was mortified when she later realised that she was dragging Jenni out of the bathroom, telling her not to worry and that it would all be okay!

Of course, there was the trauma of getting myself bald - or as close to it as possible. We took the kids to the barber's shop so that it wouldn't be a surprise to them... but I chose not to invite them to the chest waxing :-)
