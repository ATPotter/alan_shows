In 1983, a callow youth of just sixteen years, I performed in "The Zoo" and "The Pirates of Penzance" with the Glasgow Savoy Club. It was my first show outside of school.

Imagine my surprise when, after a fairly lengthy introduction, we started singing. I kid you not - we spent over twenty minutes on the word "Hail". And then more than ten minutes on "Po". Just before tea-time, we got to join them up, eventually this became "Hail Poetry"

I don't imagine there are many clubs where things are taken to that level of detail these days!

I remember being stunned by the quality and power of the voices there. It was the first time I had ever been unable to hear myself when I was singing, the singers around me were so good. And then came Kathryn Lloyd-Evans, a superb soprano who made Mabel sound both amazingly powerful and ridiculously easy. I was absolutely knocked out - she was superb!
