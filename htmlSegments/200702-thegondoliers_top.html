In October 2006 I was becoming seriously concerned about whether I would be able to find a show to do over the winter time. I had had a couple of societies in mind, but it turned out that they were both doing shows that would be inapproriate to take the children along to see. I couldn't think of what to do.

One Friday evening I was shopping in Tesco, South Queensferry, when my phone rang. The caller wondered if I might be willing to help Dunfermlin Gilbert and Sullivan Society, who were trying to cast Luiz in The Gondoliers? After some thought, I realised that this would work perfectly.

I had fond memories of the Society, having done Princess Ida there in 1992, and indeed there were still a few folk that I recognised there. And Peter Macfarlane had directed SABOS for a couple of shows too, so I felt quickly at home.

Luiz is an unexpectedly small role; he is only on with the chorus at the finalé. As a result this was quite a strange show to do, as I knew the principals I was working with but hardly anyone else in the company. Of course, by the time that show week had come and gone I knew everyone, but it was a little strange until then.

I also have to say that it was one of the best-organised companies I have ever been in. Being able to leave your shirt in a bag and come in to find it washed overnight is simply luxury!

Photographic Notes

This was something of a revelation, photographically speaking.

I had bought a Canon EOS350D digital SLR, and frankly it wasn't my friend. Outdoors, I just could not get photographs that were anywhere near as clear and sharp as those with my Pro1. I had returned it to be checked, and was told it was working just as expected. I was totally lost.

And then I borrowed a colleague's 70-200mm f/4.0 L lens, and the friend who took photographs of the show used it. Now, it was arguably a little on the long side for the Carnegie hall, but the colour reproduction and the sharpness of the photographs took my breath away. In one set of pictures it was instantly clear what the difference was - Canon's L-series glass.

As soon as I could afford it, I went out and bought a Canon 24-105mm f/4.0 L lens, and went out to play with it. Soon thereafter I started StagePics, where I was able to practise stage photography. I won't say that I've got it right, but I now understand dramatically more than I did before!
